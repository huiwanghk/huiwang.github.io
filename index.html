About

Hui is a second-year PhD candidate at the Department of Geography, The University of Hong Kong. Under the expert guidance of Professor Becky P.Y. Loo and with the invaluable support of The Joint Lab on Future Cities, he is exploring sustainable transport solutions to ensure the sustainability of future cities. Hui received the Master's degree in Transport Engineering from the College of Transportation Engineering, Tongji University in June 2022 and the B.E. degree in Railway Transport Engineering from the School of Traffic and Transportation, Beijing Jiaotong University June 2019, respectively. He has been awarded several honors including the China National Scholarship, Outstanding Graduate Award of Shanghai, Outstanding Master's Thesis award.

His research focuses on developing new transport and geography approaches (e.g., agent-based simulation, spatial-temporal data analysis method, discrete choice method) to facilitate safe, inclusive, efficient and healthy transport systems, contributing transformative changes to next-generation mobility.

Research Interests:
※ Public Transport Accessibility
※ Demand Responsive Transit
※ Metro Operation and Management
※ Road Safety and Policy
※ Transport and Public Health
