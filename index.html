About

Hui is a second-year PhD candidate at the Department of Geography, The University of Hong Kong. Under the expert guidance of Professor Becky P.Y. Loo and with the invaluable support of The Joint Lab on Future Cities, he is exploring sustainable transport solutions to ensure the sustainability of future cities. Hui received his Master's degree in Transport Engineering from the College of Transportation Engineering, at Tongji University in June 2022 and the B.E. degree in Railway Transport Engineering from the School of Traffic and Transportation, at Beijing Jiaotong University in June 2019, respectively. He has been awarded several honors, including the China National Scholarship, Outstanding Graduate Award of Shanghai, and Outstanding Master's Thesis award.

His research focuses on developing new transport and geography approaches, such as agent-based simulation, spatial-temporal data analysis methods, and discrete choice methods, to facilitate safe, inclusive, efficient and healthy transport systems, contributing transformative changes to next-generation mobility.

Projects

Public Transport Disadvantage

The Public Transport Disadvantaged in a Highly Transit-oriented City.

Accomplishments
  • A holistic framework to identify public transport disadvantaged areas.
  • Public transport accessibility to a broad range of public facilities is considered.
  • Walking to transit stops, not waiting for vehicles, is a rural transport problem.
  • Rural areas struggle to access education, healthcare, & shopping through public transport.
  • Rural areas need 90+ minutes for similar opportunities as urban areas within 30 minutes.
  • A differentiated approach to improving accessibility in transport disadvantaged areas.
Station Time Consumption

Dynamics of In-Station Time within Metro Systems.

Accomplishments
  • an agent-based simulation to measure the dynamics of in-station time.
  • explores three different influential factors and their effects on in-station time.
  • highest in-station time exceeds more than 25 minutes.
  • emphasizing the importance of responsive measures, prioritizing inbound and transfer passenger flows.
  • informing responsive solutions to reduce time consumption in metro stations.
Demand Responsive Transit

Adaptability evaluation method of Route Deviation Demand Responsive Transit.

Accomplishments
  • Summarizes the research methods of domestic and foreign DRT adaptability.
  • A techno-economic assessment method is used to support the analysis of the RdDRT adaptability.
  • A simulation modeling of RdDRT is created to support for the technical and economic evaluation model.
  • Selecting Shanghai Jiading No. 66 bus as the research case.

Travel

My travels have taken me to a diverse array of cities. As a student of transport and geography, I've developed a deep appreciation for both urban landscapes and natural wonders. I find joy in exploring city streets, observing how urban design intertwines with transport systems to shape daily life. Equally, I'm drawn to the serenity of forests and the vastness of oceans, often venturing outdoors to experience nature's beauty firsthand.

Through my geographical perspective, I've come to see these urban spaces as more than just points on a map. Each city tells its own story, with differences in planning, architecture, and culture that continue to fascinate me.

I've created a map to illustrate this journey: red markers indicate places where I've lived for extended periods, absorbing the local culture and rhythms; green markers highlight cities I've explored as a curious visitor; and blue markers point to destinations that spark my imagination for future adventures.
